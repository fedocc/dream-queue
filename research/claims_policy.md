# Claims Policy

Dream Queue AI is a research MVP and pilot proposal. Public copy, README text, pitch material, and UI labels must distinguish evidence from assumptions and model outputs.

## Provenance Labels

Use one of these labels for every material claim or number:

| Label | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Directly supported by a public source or repo research artifact. | Genting VQ reservations are linked to entrance tickets. |
| Assumption | A modeling or pilot assumption selected before real park data exists. | `no_show_rate`, `route_compliance_rate`, paid capacity cap. |
| Model output | Produced by the local simulation or financial model. | Simulated wait-time change under a configured scenario. |
| Pilot hypothesis | A claim to test in a 4-8 week pilot. | Freed wait time may shift some guest activity to rides, shows, F&B, or retail. |

## Allowed Claims

- Public Genting/Alibaba evidence confirms that VQ, itinerary planning, incentives, crowd prediction, QR validation, group reservations, kiosks, and late-window policy are real theme-park operating patterns.
- Dream Queue does not increase physical ride capacity; it manages guest time, slot inventory, route guidance, and operational risk.
- Dream Island / "Остров Мечты" has public evidence of many attractions, shows, express entry, and VIP-express. This supports a pilot conversation, not a proven pain diagnosis.
- China smart-tourism evidence supports flexible reservation systems, offline access, lower PII, and anti-scalper controls.
- Vendor materials from accesso, Attractions.io, and Lineberty show that virtual queue and operator tooling are mature categories. Treat vendor KPIs as vendor claims unless independently audited.

## Forbidden Claims

- Alibaba/Genting proved Dream Queue revenue uplift.
- Virtual queue eliminates queues.
- Simulation outputs are measured park performance.
- All guests will follow route recommendations.
- Paid priority slots can be sold without standby degradation.
- Dream Island has specific wait times, queue complaints, no-show rates, or revenue leakage unless sourced or measured.
- Any KPI, conversion rate, capacity number, or operational dataset not present in the repo or source list.

## Copy Rules

- Prefer "pilot", "scenario", "assumption", "category evidence", and "model output" over "proven", "guaranteed", or "validated".
- When discussing Genting/Alibaba, include both what it proves and what it does not prove.
- When discussing Dream Island, frame the product as a low-risk pilot around selected bottlenecks, not full-park AI automation.
- When showing model numbers, state the input assumptions or link to the relevant config/output file.
- If a source is unclear, downgrade the claim to assumption or remove it.

## Genting/Alibaba Wording

Safe wording:

> Public evidence from Genting/Alibaba confirms the operating category: VQ + itinerary planning + incentives + crowd prediction in a theme-park setting. It does not disclose a guaranteed revenue uplift, wait-time reduction, algorithmic accuracy, no-show rate, or implementation cost.
