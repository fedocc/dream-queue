# Evidence Map

Dream Queue AI is a research MVP and pilot proposal, not a proven production product. This map defines how public evidence can be used in the product narrative without turning category proof into unsupported economics.

## Provenance Labels

| Label | Use for | Product wording |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Public source or repo research artifact. | "Genting links VQ reservations to entrance tickets." |
| Assumption | Modeling or pilot input selected before park data exists. | "`no_show_rate` and paid-cap limits are assumptions until measured." |
| Model output | Local simulation or financial model result. | "Scenario output under configured assumptions." |
| Pilot hypothesis | Claim to test with real park data. | "Freed wait time may shift some guest activity to rides, shows, F&B, or retail." |

## Evidence Blocks

| Evidence block | What we know | What we do not know | Safe Dream Queue use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Genting SkyWorlds + Alibaba Cloud | Public materials describe VQ, itinerary planning, incentive recommendations, crowd analysis/prediction, ticket-linked QR validation, group booking, kiosks, availability limits, and late-window rules. | No disclosed revenue uplift, wait-time reduction, model accuracy, implementation cost, no-show rate, or recommendation compliance. | Category proof for a VQ + routing + operations pattern. Not proof of Dream Queue economics. |
| Alibaba smart scenic area / Universal Beijing pattern | Alibaba frames theme-park/scenic operations as guest journey plus data infrastructure: entry, route, queue, food, retail, payment, and operations. | Whether the same full-stack scope is appropriate for Dream Island or a first pilot. | Borrow the architecture logic in lightweight form: slot inventory, wait visibility, routing, dashboard. Avoid biometrics and heavy PII. |
| China smart-tourism policy and market evidence | High-crowd leisure markets use reservations, real-time visitor-flow tools, digital scenic platforms, offline counters, simplified procedures, reduced PII burden, and anti-scalper controls. | Exact transferability to Moscow demand or Dream Island operations. | Design hybrid access: app/web plus kiosk/staff path, standby fallback, anti-hoarding rules, and minimum PII. |
| accesso, Attractions.io, Lineberty | Vendor materials show virtual queuing, operator tooling, QR/ticket validation, queue controls, no-show/abandon tracking, group handling, terminal fallback, and dashboards are mature category features. | Vendor KPI claims are not independent audited evidence unless separately verified. | Use as category and feature-pattern evidence. Do not repeat vendor revenue/minutes-saved claims as facts. |
| Disney virtual queue | Public rules emphasize limited availability, return windows, group/ticket logic, operational exceptions, and no guarantee of immediate access. | Disney does not prove Dream Queue revenue, guest compliance, or wait-time effect. | Make scarcity and rules visible: VQ manages access windows; it does not eliminate queues. |
| Dream Island public facts | Public pages support that the park has many attractions/shows and existing express/VIP priority products. Public material also supports differentiated pricing and expansion context. | Real wait times, no-show rates, attraction throughput, guest complaints at operational scale, paid conversion, and standby impact. | Frame a focused 4-8 week pilot on 5-8 suspected bottleneck attractions and peak windows, with baseline measurement first. |

## Narrative Guardrail

Safe core narrative:

> Public evidence confirms the operating category: virtual queue slots, wait prediction, guest routing, validation, offline fallback, risk controls, and limited priority inventory. Dream Queue AI proposes a smaller pilot layer for Dream Island, with every result labelled as Source, Assumption, Model output, or Pilot hypothesis.

Unsafe narrative:

> Genting or Alibaba proved Dream Queue will increase revenue or remove queues at Dream Island.

## Pilot Evidence To Collect

The pilot should start by separating what is measured from what is modeled:

- baseline wait-time observations on selected rides;
- attraction capacity and dispatch assumptions;
- downtime and late-arrival observations;
- existing express/VIP constraints;
- slot utilization and no-show handling;
- guest/staff feedback on app, kiosk, and standby paths;
- aggregated F&B/retail proxy only if the park can share it safely;
- before/after comparison clearly labelled as measured, survey, or model output.
