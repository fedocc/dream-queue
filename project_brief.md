# Project Brief

## Name

Dream Queue AI - research MVP and pilot proposal for queue-flow intelligence, virtual slots, guest routing, risk controls, and limited priority inventory in amusement parks.

## One-liner

We help amusement parks test whether virtual slots, load forecasting, guest routing, and governed priority inventory can improve queue-flow operations.

## Case Study

Dream Island, Moscow.

## Positioning

The MVP is not a replacement for park infrastructure. It is an analytical layer and simulation prototype for testing controlled queue inventory, demand redistribution, risk controls, and scenario economics.

## Core MVP Questions

- How often do guests complain about queues and crowding?
- Which attractions are likely to create bottlenecks?
- What scenario revenue could paid priority slots create under explicit assumptions and capacity caps?
- Can route recommendations reduce waiting time without increasing physical capacity?
- What operator dashboard would make this useful for park managers?

## Pitch Angle

Physical waiting may create opportunity cost when guests spend less time in rides, shows, food, retail, games, and extra entertainment. Dream Queue AI turns that into a measurable pilot hypothesis rather than a pre-claimed result.

## Near-Term Pitch Direction

Use Genting SkyWorlds + Alibaba Cloud as a category benchmark: public evidence describes AI-powered Virtual Queue, itinerary planning, crowd prediction, and incentives. It does not disclose Dream Queue economics or a guaranteed KPI.

For Dream Island, the first offer should be lighter:

- analyze current public evidence and pain points;
- model likely bottlenecks;
- simulate baseline vs optimized guest flow;
- estimate fast-slot revenue scenarios;
- show an operator dashboard prototype;
- propose a low-risk 4-8 week pilot before deep integration.
